Subtle, critiqued

I read through the archive and asked: what's uniquely good here, what holds it back, and how can Subtle level up over the next 60 days?

What’s working

  • Clear POV: solo-first, agentic software, practical shipping. The voice is confident without sounding theoretical.
  • Cadence and recency: steady, timely posts tied to real products and current events.
  • Hands-on credibility: posts like Agentic Equities and stringme.dev show work, not takes.

Gaps

  • Navigation/IA: the homepage is a long scroll. Readers can’t jump to themes (e.g., pricing, one-person ops, evaluations).
  • Evergreen packaging: great sequences exist but aren’t packaged (e.g., PRD/evals/guardrails could be a mini playbook).
  • Lead capture: the email module exists but isn’t contextually triggered or measured. No social previews for top series.
  • Post SEO: sitemap is stale and misses many URLs; some titles/OG descriptions are generic; several pages lack internal links.

60‑day roadmap

  1. Create topic hubs: add a /topics/ page and tag each post in front matter-like JSON inside a <script type="application/json" id="post-meta"> block. Render hub pages with client-side JS.
  2. Package 3 playbooks: Ship three evergreen guides: (1) Agentic pipeline design, (2) Pricing and approval thresholds, (3) LLM evals for PMs. Each gets a short PDF lead magnet.
  3. Fix SEO plumbing: auto‑generate sitemap.xml from the folder list via a small script; ensure every post has unique meta, canonical, and 1–2 internal links.
  4. Email growth: add inline subscribe CTAs after the 2nd section of long posts; fire an event to Plausible; A/B test copy weekly.
  5. Social cards: add a lightweight image generator for OG cards with post title/date; store under /public/og/.

Quick wins (this week)

  • Update sitemap.xml with the latest 20 posts.
  • Add internal links from newer posts back to cornerstone pieces.
  • Normalize post titles to a concise pattern and tighten descriptions to 150–160 chars.

Quality bar

  • One strong chart per piece: even a simple p95 latency or $/task plot makes posts more referenceable.
  • Proof over proclamations: ship a toy or dataset with each claim when feasible.
  • Named concepts: keep coining sticky phrases (e.g., “vibe-coded landing page”). Create an index.

Subtle’s niche is strong. With a few IA and packaging upgrades, the archive becomes a product—easy to navigate, easy to cite, and easy to subscribe to.
